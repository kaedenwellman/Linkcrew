So what actually is Link Crew?

Upperclassmen who show up for the new kids.

A group of ACP upperclassmen gets trained to run events that help freshmen actually settle in, not just survive orientation, but find their footing. It's part program, part mentorship, and part just showing up and being someone the next generation of Knights can count on.

It's also part of a bigger network. Link Crew runs at high schools across the country, which means you're plugged into something that's been figured out over a long time, and you get to make it your own at ACP.

The big events

What you'll spend your time doing.

Mandatory

Orientation Day

You'll run small-group activities with incoming freshmen on their very first day at ACP. Games that feel fun but are quietly teaching something real. By the end, everyone in your group will have laughed, learned names, and talked to at least one person they wouldn't have otherwise.

Ongoing

Engagement Series

After orientation, you keep showing up. Campus events, community activities, the Fall Carnival. Your job is to make sure the freshmen you met on Day 1 still feel connected to something when October rolls around. The relationship doesn't end after the first week.

What's in it for you

You'll get more out of this than you think.

Running real events, reading a room, earning trust from people who didn't ask to be in your group and watching it work anyway. That's not something you can fake on a college app. Link Crew teaches you how to actually bring people together, and that ends up mattering long after high school.

Event 01 / Mandatory

Orientation Day

Picture this: it's the first day of high school. You don't know a single person in the room. You get randomly placed in a group with strangers. And then some upperclassman walks in who actually seems happy to be there, runs a few activities, and somehow makes the whole thing feel less terrifying.

That's you. That's the job.

Your group moves through activities that look like games but are built around something real: learning names, building trust, stepping into something new without the people you already know as a safety net. You're not there to lecture. You're there to be the kind of person who makes it easier to walk into high school.

The Ball Juggling Game

A crowd favorite, and the one that lands hardest. Press Start to see how it plays out, then add balls to feel it escalate.

Students stand in a circle. They've just learned each other's names.

By the time you're juggling three balls, everyone's laughing and slightly panicking. That's when you stop and say: that's what high school feels like. Lots of things in the air at once. The trick is figuring out how to keep them moving together.

What Leaders Do

  • Set a warm, low-pressure tone from the moment you walk in
  • Run each activity clearly, then talk about the message behind it
  • Keep energy high, especially when students are hesitant
  • Connect the game back to real high school life
  • Make sure every freshman knows you're a familiar face on campus now
Event 02 / Ongoing

The Engagement Series

Orientation Day is the introduction. The Engagement Series is the follow-through.

These are optional events spread throughout the year: campus activities, community stuff, things that give your freshmen a reason to keep showing up past the first week. The whole point is that Link Crew doesn't disappear after Day 1.

One example: the Fall Carnival. Your chapter runs a game booth where students use carnival tickets to play. This year it was a ring toss on soda bottles. Land the ring, win the bottle. Everyone throws from the same line. Except freshmen, who get to throw from closer. The "freshman advantage."

It sounds small, but it works. Every time a freshman asks why their line is closer, that's your opening. You get to tell them what Link Crew is, what it's given you, and why they should think about applying when the time comes.

You also personally reach out to your freshmen and invite them. Not a flyer, an actual message from you. It makes a difference.

What Leaders Do

  • Personally invite your freshmen. A message from you lands differently than a flyer
  • Use the freshman advantage as a natural recruiting conversation
  • Show up consistently so they see Link Crew as ongoing, not a one-time thing
  • Push them gently past their comfort zone with each new event
  • Plant the seed for the next generation of ACP Link Crew leaders
The part nobody sees

What makes it all actually happen.

Events feel easy when they're running well. That's because a lot of work happened before anyone showed up.

Planning & Organizing

Every event starts weeks out. Someone maps the dates, the theme, the setup, what leaders need to know. When you walk in, everything's already in motion and you can focus on your group.

Supplies & Donations

Poster paper, markers, food for events, game materials, decorations — every event has a list of things it needs. Sra. Michaelson coordinates sourcing and donations so leaders can focus on showing up and doing their part.

Multi-School Coordination

ACP's chapter doesn't exist in a bubble. Sra. Michaelson connects with staff and leaders from other schools, shares what's working, and brings ideas back that make our events better every year.
